02THE SETUP

What happened — the drawdown story

AI
This section reconstructs the drawdown narrative from public sources. It is not the author's view — that's §3.

NKE peaked above $80 in spring 2024 on hopes that the John Donahoe-era pivot to direct-to-consumer would deliver the next leg of growth. The reversal began with the FY24 Q4 print (June 2024): management guided FY25 revenue down low-single-digits, signaling that the post-COVID consumer-direct momentum had snapped. The board lost faith in Donahoe and brought back Nike lifer Elliott Hill in October 2024 — a 32-year company veteran who had retired in 2020, recalled to lead the comeback.

FY25 (May 2024–May 2025) confirmed the operational damage: revenue fell 9.8% to $46.31B, the first decline of that magnitude in two decades outside of COVID. Net income collapsed 43% to $3.22B; FCF dropped 51% to $3.27B; capex was slashed 47% to $0.43B as Hill prioritized cash defense over reinvestment offense. The narrative crystallized around three points: (1) the Donahoe-era over-reliance on Nike Direct alienated wholesale partners (DICK's, Foot Locker, JD) at the exact moment they were gaining share; (2) classic franchise saturation (Air Force 1, Dunk, Air Max) — products had over-shipped, gone "ugly" through over-distribution, and lost cultural relevance; (3) specialist brands — On Running, Hoka, New Balance, Salomon, Tracksmith — captured running and lifestyle share faster than incumbents recognized.

FY26 (the current year, ending May 2026) has stabilized topline at flat YoY but compressed margins further. Q3 FY26 (reported March 31, 2026) showed Q3 revenue of $11.28B — flat reported, -3% currency-neutral — with gross margin contracting 130 basis points to 40.2%. CFO Matt Friend disclosed that 300 basis points of the GM compression came from elevated U.S. tariffs alone, and an additional $230M one-time severance charge sat in SG&A. Q3 EPS landed at $0.35, a -35% YoY decline. Hill stated explicitly that "Win Now" inventory cleanup — intentionally removing $4 billion of revenue from peak classic-footwear levels — would be complete by end of calendar 2026, with margin inflection targeted for Q2 FY27 (Sept-Nov 2026).

April 24, 2026 brought the latest cost-reset chapter: Nike announced 1,400 tech-team layoffs (approximately 2% of headcount), centralizing technology to Beaverton + Nike India Tech Center, following 775 warehouse cuts in January. Q4 FY26 guidance: -2% to -4% revenue decline; Greater China -20%; modest North America growth offset by China + Converse drags.

The market is pricing this as: A multi-year structural reset. Forward P/E of 23.95× sits on a depressed numerator (FY26 EPS $1.50, down from $2.16) — the math that price implies a slow, halting recovery rather than rapid V-shape. Sell-side has converged at $48–53 price targets with a $23 BNP Paribas outlier. The dividend-yield support around 3.7% is becoming the floor of the range.

03CORE QUESTION

Short-term or structural?

AI
The core analytical question. Every memo's value is downstream of this judgment.
🐂 Short-term case
  1. Revenue stabilized at roughly flat YoY across all three FY26 quarters (Q1 -0.4%, Q2 +0.6%, Q3 +0.1% reported). The bleeding stopped.
  2. March 2026 insider cluster buy at the lows: CEO Hill, Director Tim Cook, Director Swan, Director Rogers — coordinated, high-signal purchases by senior insiders, not random Form 4 noise.
  3. Sport offense is producing measurable wins: Nike Running +20% in Q3 currency-neutral; Vomero 18 generated $100M in 3 months; new platforms (Nike Mind, Aero-FIT, liquid Air Max) sold out across geographies.
  4. Wholesale +1% in Q3 — DICK's, Foot Locker, JD, Academy partnerships strengthening after Donahoe-era damage. Hill explicitly committed to the integrated-marketplace model.
  5. Quality long-onlys adding: Capital World Investors +16.17% position growth, Wellington Management +56.28% in the latest reported quarter — concentrated active managers, not indexers.
  6. Balance sheet funds patience: $6.66B cash, ~zero net debt, $3.27B FY25 FCF still covers the $1.97B annual dividend with room for buybacks.
🐻 Structural case
  1. Greater China guided to a -20% revenue decline after Q3's -7% to $1.62B. Magnitude suggests structural Chinese-consumer rejection of US athletic brands, not cyclical destocking. Anta and Li-Ning continue to take domestic share.
  2. Brand share loss to specialists is real and accelerating: On Running, Hoka (DECK), New Balance (private, $7B+ revenue), Salomon (Amer Sports), Adidas Samba/Gazelle/Sambazo. The Sportswear/lifestyle category fell -low double-digits in Q3.
  3. Sentiment trough not confirmed: 21 down vs 7 up FY26 EPS revisions in past 30 days; 13 down vs 1 up for Q1 FY27. BNP Paribas at $23 PT and Underperform is a serious tail-risk anchor.
  4. FY25 capex slashed 47% to $0.43B, FY26 capex tracking similarly defensive — this is cost-cutting, not reinvestment offense. Quality compounders need to see CapEx going up under the new CEO, not down.
  5. Talent attrition signal: Lululemon hired Heidi O'Neill (long-time Nike executive) as CEO in April 2026. When competitors pull your senior brand talent at the moment of your turnaround, that is rarely a coincidence.
  6. Forward P/E 23.95× on a depressed numerator is not deep-value (vs. 12-15× for cyclical-trough consumer names). The market is not pricing this as a layup.
Verdict
Mixed (leans Short-term) · 12–18 month recovery window
Confidence: Medium

Revenue has stabilized; insider conviction is unambiguous; the sport offense is producing real product traction (Running +20%, Vomero, Mind 001). These are short-term-reset signals. Greater China and the Sportswear/lifestyle share loss to specialists are real structural overhangs that gate position size. The Hill turnaround has the balance sheet, the credibility (32 years inside), and the visible internal traction to be valued as a 12-18 month earnings inflection — but the gate-failure (drawdown 44% vs 50% threshold) plus the unconfirmed China stabilization keep this short of high-conviction.

Biggest uncertainty: Whether the guided -20% Greater China revenue decline reflects cyclical destocking (Hill's framing) or a permanent shift in Chinese consumer preference toward domestic brands. Q1 FY27 print (September 2026) is the next clean read. If China narrows to -10% or better, thesis tightens materially toward Short-term; if it widens to -25%+, thesis flips toward Structural and exit triggers fire.

06BUSINESS & MOAT

Business & competitive moat

AI
Moat analysis. Output of a "could a well-funded entrant replicate this in 3 years?" mental test.

NIKE designs, develops, markets, and sells athletic and casual footwear, apparel, equipment, and accessories under the Nike, Jordan, and Converse brands across 190 countries. Distribution runs through wholesale (DICK's, Foot Locker, JD Sports, Academy, etc.), Nike Direct (700+ owned retail stores worldwide), and digital (Nike.com + apps). FY25 revenue $46.3B; 77,800 employees globally; HQ in Beaverton, Oregon.

Moat components

  • Brand (highest moat). Nike is the most-valuable single sport brand globally — ~50 years of brand-building, the Jordan sub-brand alone is a $7B+ business, and the Swoosh logo carries cultural weight no specialist can match. This is the load-bearing wall of the thesis.
  • Athlete & team partnerships. Endorsement contracts with the world's top performers (LeBron, Caitlin Clark, Coco Gauff) plus team kit deals (90+ federations for World Cup 2026, Premier League clubs). Retention switches are durable — teenagers who form preference watching their idol in Nikes don't churn cheaply.
  • R&D + IP scale. Q3 FY26 disclosed: Nike Mind platform with 150+ patents filed globally, Aero-FIT cooling, liquid Air Max. Innovation pipeline visible 2-3 years out (CEO referenced reviewing 2027/2028 agenda by sport). Specialists like On have a tighter category focus but lack the cross-platform scaling that Nike's labs enable.
  • Distribution scale. 190 countries, multi-channel (wholesale + DTC + digital), $46B revenue base. Replicating this physical reach is impossible in any reasonable horizon.
  • Manufacturing scale. Decade-plus relationships with Vietnamese / Indonesian / Indian factories give NKE unit-cost advantages that smaller specialists can't match at any volume.

Moat vulnerabilities

  • Brand affinity is generational. If Gen Z forms preference for On, Hoka, New Balance, Salomon, Tracksmith, etc. at age 14-22, that becomes structural over a decade. Piper Sandler teen survey trends in 2024-2025 showed Nike still #1 but with measurable erosion vs. specialists.
  • Lifestyle / streetwear category. Adidas Samba/Gazelle resurgence + New Balance 9060 / 530 + ASICS terrace running + Salomon trail-running take Nike Sportswear share at exactly the highest-margin end of the assortment.
  • China is a separate competitive battlefield. Anta and Li-Ning have invested heavily in domestic-brand positioning, and Chinese consumer preference has shifted toward local brands across multiple categories. This is a structural problem, not an inventory problem.
  • Wholesale partner leverage was damaged. The Donahoe-era DTC pivot alienated DICK's, Foot Locker, and JD; even with Hill's repair work, partner trust is multi-year to fully restore.

Replicability test: A well-funded entrant could not replicate the Nike or Jordan brand in 3 years. They could replicate any single product. Brand is the moat, and within that brand, Jordan is the deepest sub-moat. The current question is not whether the moat exists — it does — but whether it is being eroded at the edges (lifestyle, Greater China) faster than core (performance, North America) regrows.

07MACRO & SECTOR

Macro & sector context

Athletic footwear & apparel category. Global TAM ~$370B, growing mid-single-digits. Specialist running brands (On, Hoka, Salomon, New Balance) growing 20-40% off smaller bases; lifestyle segment (Adidas Samba, NB 9060, Asics) reflowing share from Nike legacy classics. Athleisure overall has cooled from 8-10% growth (2020-2023) to LSD globally as the post-COVID closet-saturation effect plays out.

US tariffs — material headwind. Q3 FY26 gross margin compressed 130 bps total, of which 300 bps came from elevated US tariffs in North America alone (per CFO Matt Friend on the March 31, 2026 earnings call). NKE sources approximately 50% of footwear from Vietnam, ~25% from Indonesia, ~10% from China, and remainder from India / other Asian countries. Continued US tariff escalation is the single biggest exogenous risk to FY26-27 margin recovery.

Greater China. Q3 FY26 revenue $1.62B (-7% YoY); guided to a -20% revenue decline in subsequent quarters. The drivers: (1) reduced sell-in to clean the marketplace; (2) domestic brand ascendance (Anta, Li-Ning have gained share in athletic categories across all tiers); (3) macro consumer weakness in China across discretionary. Hill's framing on the Q3 call: "structural challenges in China and channel dynamics" — language acknowledging this is more than cyclical destocking.

Currency. USD strength has been a 100-200bps headwind to reported revenue; reversal possible if Fed cuts, but not load-bearing for the thesis.

Catalyst calendar exogenous to NKE. The 2026 FIFA World Cup (June-July 2026) is hosted across US/Canada/Mexico, with massive Nike presence — Nike federation kits including Brazil (Jordan-branded away kit), Mercurial boot launch in June, and 5,000+ activated football retail doors globally. The 2028 LA Olympics is the next major catalyst. NKE's NBA All-Star Weekend execution in February 2026 was framed by management as the template for World Cup / Super Bowl / Olympics activations.

Sector-wide layoff cycle. NKE's 1,400-job April 2026 cut sits within a broader retail/tech layoff wave: IKEA 800, Amazon 16K (Jan 2026 round), eBay 800, Ocado 1,000. The cuts are not idiosyncratic — they reflect a category-wide cost reset post-pandemic over-investment.

08CAPITAL STRUCTURE

Capital structure & capital returns

  • Debt profile. Long-term debt $7.03B (noncurrent, Q3 FY26); cash $6.66B → effectively zero net leverage. Investment-grade credit, no near-term refinancing strain.
  • Dividend. $1.64 annual ($0.41/quarter), yielding 3.67% at $44.69. FY25 FCF $3.27B comfortably covers $1.97B annual dividend even at trough — coverage ratio ~1.66×.
  • Buybacks. Historically a major capital-return lever (~$5B/year peak). Likely paused or reduced in FY26 given the cleanup, but not zero.
  • M&A. NKE has historically been a sub-scale acquirer — Converse (2003) is the only meaningful purchase. No M&A activity expected during turnaround.
  • CapEx. FY25 $0.43B (cut −47% YoY from $0.81B). FY26 tracking similarly defensive. This is the line that quality-compounder investors (Akre, Polen) flag as a yellow card — turnaround CEOs should be increasing CapEx, not cutting it.
  • Class A controlling stake. The Knight family and trusts control Class A shares with super-voting rights — meaningful constraint on classic activist intervention. Pershing Square / Trian-style activism is structurally limited at NKE.

Read. Capital structure is a tailwind for the thesis. Zero-net-debt balance sheet, dividend yield 3.67% setting a soft price floor, and FCF that covers the dividend through trough. The CapEx cut is the only flag — it favors a defensive, cash-preserving turnaround over an offensive reinvestment-led one. If Hill commits to higher FY27 CapEx at the fall Investor Day, that meaningfully tightens the bull case.

Fundamental principles for judging Consumer Discretionary

Synthesized across all 8 firms' public commentary into a durable checklist. These are the timeless tests these investors apply to any consumer brand stock — not the latest opinion. Click any firm chip to open its source.

01Brand durability requires product reinvention every 5–7 years
Premium consumer brands lose Gen Z formation if product line stays static. Brand strength data leads revenue data by 2–3 years — by the time revenue softens, the brand-perception decline is already 2 years old. NKE's "Win Now" reset is precisely the kind of forced reinvention that the L Catterton playbook treats as either rebirth or terminal.
Champions: L Catterton↗ Forerunner↗ Maveron↗
02Gross margin is the truest read on brand health
Premium-priced brands lose unit volume disproportionately in a downturn — but recover faster on the upturn if gross margin holds. Margin compression precedes revenue decline as a leading indicator of brand erosion. NKE's Q3 FY26 GM at 40.2% (-130 bps) is the central data point — but stripping out the 300 bps of US tariff impact, the underlying GM is closer to 43%, materially less alarming.
Champions: Verlinvest↗ Pershing Square↗
03Specialists win distribution against legacy incumbents
Brand-as-distribution is structurally won by specialists who own the customer relationship directly. Legacy retail incumbents face a 5–10 year displacement curve in any healthy category. NKE's wholesale rebuild (DICK's, Foot Locker, JD partnerships) is a tacit acknowledgment that pure-DTC was wrong; whether it can defend the floor against On / Hoka / Salomon is the open question.
Champions: Forerunner↗ L Catterton↗
04Quality compounders > trading
High-ROIC consumer brands with clear reinvestment runway compound for decades. Don't trade in/out on quarterly noise — over multi-year horizons, the cost of being wrong on timing exceeds the gain of being right. NKE checks the brand and category boxes; the open question is reinvestment runway when CapEx has been cut −47%.
Champions: Akre Capital↗ Polen Capital↗
05International is the second-derivative growth lever — until it isn't
When the home market saturates, international is where compounding continues. Watch international as % of mix AND growth rate. For NKE, Greater China going from growth engine to -20% headwind is the inverse of this principle — the lever that built the FY15-22 thesis is now actively breaking it.
Champions: Lerer Hippeau↗ Verlinvest↗
06Repeat purchase > marketing buzz
Trailing-12-month customer cohort retention is the truest indicator of brand health vs. one-time hype. New-customer acquisition without retention is not growth — it's value destruction at premium CAC. NKE doesn't disclose cohort retention publicly, but Nike Membership's 350M+ member base and the wholesale order-book growth Hill cited in Q3 are the proxies.
